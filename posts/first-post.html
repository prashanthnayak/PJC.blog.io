  • The period between two consecutive conjunctions of the Sun and Moon (amāvāsya) or their opposition – pūrṇimā, takes about 29.5 days1
  • Every tithi is an angular displacement between the Sun and Moon measuring 12°.
  • Every day the Moon travels about 13° while the Sun moves ahead by 1°
  • These 30 tithi can be divided into two parts based on the waxing and waning Moon phases. The waxing phase is called śukla (bright) pakṣa while the waning phase is called kṛṣṇa pakṣa
  • The śloka teaches that śukla pakṣa is associated (or lorded) by the deva while the kṛṣṇa pakṣa is lorded by the pitṛs.
  • 1) In the northern hemisphere, if the left side of the Moon is dark then it is śukla pakṣa while if the right side of the Moon is dark then it is kṛṣṇa pakṣa. Reverse for southern hemisphere. (2) If neither side is dark and it is very circular then it is a full moon, else if there is no moon in the sky that night, then it is amāvāsya or śukla pratipada. (3) If you can see a crescent then it is a weak moon seeking protection by showing its horns while if the face shows (bulging moon) then it is happy and strong.
